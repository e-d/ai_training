## The Building Blocks of Modern AI

Understanding the technologies behind AI helps you evaluate vendor claims, ask better questions, and identify which solutions fit your specific problems. This lesson covers the four key categories you'll encounter most often: natural language processing, computer vision, generative AI, and robotics/RPA.

## Natural Language Processing (NLP)

Natural language processing is AI's ability to understand, interpret, and generate human language — both text and speech. Modern NLP uses transformer models (the architecture behind GPT and BERT) that learn language patterns from billions of text examples. NLP is the technology behind every chatbot, translation service, and voice assistant you've used.

- **Customer service chatbots:** Zendesk reports that AI-powered chatbots now resolve 65-80% of routine customer inquiries without human intervention, freeing support agents for complex issues.
- **Real-time translation:** DeepL and Google Translate use NLP models to translate between 100+ languages. Booking.com processes customer communications in 43 languages using AI translation, eliminating the need for multilingual support teams at every location.
- **Sentiment analysis and text mining:** JPMorgan's COiN platform uses NLP to review commercial loan agreements in seconds — work that previously required 360,000 hours of lawyer time annually.

## Computer Vision

Computer vision enables AI systems to interpret and act on visual information — images, video, and real-time camera feeds. These systems use neural networks trained on millions of labeled images to identify patterns, classify objects, and analyze scenes. If NLP gives AI the ability to read, computer vision gives it the ability to see.

- **Medical imaging:** AI-powered diagnostic tools can detect cancerous tumors, diabetic retinopathy, and bone fractures from scans. Google Health's mammography AI reduced false negatives by 9.4% compared to expert radiologists in a *Nature* study.
- **Autonomous vehicles:** Waymo, Tesla, and Cruise use computer vision as a core sensing technology, combining camera feeds with lidar and radar. Waymo's vehicles have logged over 20 million miles on public roads as of 2024.
- **Manufacturing quality control:** BMW uses computer vision to inspect painted surfaces for defects at production line speed. Foxconn uses similar systems to inspect electronic components at thousands per minute.

## Generative AI

Generative AI creates new content — text, images, code, audio, and video — rather than simply analyzing existing data. Large language models (LLMs) like GPT-4 and Claude predict the most likely next word in a sequence, while image models like DALL-E and Midjourney learn the relationship between text descriptions and visual elements. This category exploded into mainstream awareness with ChatGPT's launch in late 2022.

- **Content creation and marketing:** Companies use generative AI to draft marketing copy, product descriptions, and social media posts. Jasper AI reported that enterprise customers reduced content production time by 40-60% using AI-assisted writing.
- **Software development:** GitHub Copilot, an AI code assistant, is used by over 1.8 million developers and 50,000 organizations. GitHub reports that developers using Copilot complete tasks 55% faster than those who don't.
- **Design and prototyping:** Architecture firms use generative AI to produce building design variations. Pharmaceutical companies use it to generate candidate molecular structures. Adobe's Firefly generates commercial-safe images for creative teams.

## Robotics and Robotic Process Automation (RPA)

Robotics and RPA represent AI's ability to take physical or digital actions. Physical robotics combines AI with mechanical systems — robots that perceive their environment and manipulate objects. RPA is the software equivalent: bots that mimic human interactions with computer systems, processing forms and moving data between applications.

- **Warehouse automation:** Amazon operates over 750,000 robots across its fulfillment network, handling tasks from sorting packages to transporting shelves to human pickers. These robots enable Amazon to process orders 2-5 times faster than fully manual facilities.
- **Business process automation:** UiPath, a leading RPA platform, reports that enterprise customers automate an average of 30-50% of routine back-office tasks — invoice processing, employee onboarding, data entry — reducing processing time by 60-80%.
- **Surgical robotics:** Intuitive Surgical's da Vinci system has been used in over 12 million procedures worldwide. The AI-assisted platform provides surgeons with enhanced precision, 3D visualization, and tremor filtering.

> **Key takeaway:** These four technologies — NLP, computer vision, generative AI, and robotics/RPA — are the engines behind most AI applications you'll encounter. Understanding what each does helps you cut through vendor hype and match the right technology to your business problem.
