## What's Coming Next

Predicting AI's future is humbling — capabilities that seemed five years away in 2022 arrived in under two. But the trends shaping the next 3-5 years are already visible in research labs, early deployments, and regulatory frameworks. Business leaders who understand these trajectories can position their organizations to adopt emerging capabilities as they mature, rather than scrambling to catch up.

## Agentic AI: From Assistants to Autonomous Workers

The most significant near-term shift is from AI as a tool you query to AI as an agent that acts. Today's AI assistants answer questions and generate content on demand. Agentic AI plans multi-step workflows, uses external tools, makes decisions at intermediate steps, and executes end-to-end processes with minimal human intervention.

The difference is fundamental. Asking an AI "what should we do about this vendor invoice discrepancy?" gets you advice. An AI agent detects the discrepancy, pulls the original PO, compares line items, drafts a dispute email, sends it through the approval workflow, and tracks the resolution — intervening only to request human approval at predefined checkpoints.

Gartner predicts that by 2028, 33% of enterprise software will include agentic AI, up from less than 1% in 2024. Early deployments are concentrated in:

- **Software development** — Coding agents that implement features, write tests, and submit code for review
- **Customer operations** — Agents that handle end-to-end support cases, from initial triage through resolution and follow-up
- **Financial operations** — Agents that reconcile accounts, process exceptions, and flag compliance issues

The critical challenge is reliability and control. Autonomous systems need well-defined boundaries, human oversight at high-stakes decision points, and comprehensive audit trails. Organizations deploying agentic AI will need governance frameworks that didn't exist for simpler AI tools.

## Small Language Models and On-Device AI

Not every AI application needs a 100-billion-parameter model running in the cloud. Small language models (SLMs) — models with 1-7 billion parameters — are becoming remarkably capable for focused tasks while running on phones, laptops, and edge devices.

Microsoft's Phi series, Meta's Llama, and Google's Gemma demonstrate that smaller, well-trained models can match larger models on specific tasks at a fraction of the compute cost. For businesses, this enables:

- **On-device AI** — AI features that work without an internet connection, critical for field workers, manufacturing floors, and regions with unreliable connectivity
- **Data privacy** — Sensitive data never leaves the device or local network, simplifying compliance with GDPR, HIPAA, and other data residency regulations
- **Cost reduction** — Running inference on local hardware eliminates per-query API costs that scale with usage

The Stanford AI Index 2024 reports that edge AI chip shipments grew 20% year-over-year, driven by demand for on-device inference in smartphones, vehicles, and industrial equipment.

## Multimodal AI Goes Mainstream

Early AI systems excelled at one modality — text, images, or audio. Current foundation models process multiple modalities simultaneously, and the next generation will make this seamless. A customer complaint arriving as a voice call, a photo of a damaged product, and a written email will be processed as a single, unified context.

Business applications emerging from multimodal AI include:

- **Document understanding** — AI that reads contracts with embedded charts, handwritten annotations, and scanned signatures, extracting structured data from visually complex documents
- **Video analysis** — Automated review of security footage, manufacturing processes, or retail store operations at a scale that human review teams can't match
- **Rich customer interactions** — Support systems that understand a customer's spoken description, the photo they uploaded, and their account history as a single coherent context

## AI-Native Software

A new category of software is being built with AI as the core architecture, not as a feature bolted onto existing applications. These AI-native tools design around the assumption that an AI model — not a deterministic algorithm — is the primary decision engine.

Examples include:

- **Cursor and GitHub Copilot Workspace** — Development environments designed around AI pair programming, where the AI understands the full codebase and proposes multi-file changes
- **Harvey** — A legal AI platform built from the ground up for contract analysis, due diligence, and legal research, rather than adding AI to existing legal software
- **Glean** — An enterprise search platform where AI understands organizational context, permissions, and relationships between documents, rather than just matching keywords

For business leaders, AI-native tools often outperform AI features added to legacy software because the entire user experience is designed around AI capabilities and limitations.

## The Regulatory Wave

AI regulation is accelerating globally, and it will reshape how organizations deploy and govern AI systems:

- **EU AI Act** (effective August 2024) — Classifies AI systems by risk level. High-risk applications in healthcare, law enforcement, and employment face mandatory bias testing, human oversight, transparency disclosures, and documentation requirements. Fines reach 35 million euros or 7% of global revenue
- **U.S. state-level regulation** — Colorado's AI Consumer Protections Act (2024) requires disclosure when AI is used in consequential decisions. Similar bills are advancing in California, Illinois, and New York
- **China's AI regulations** — China has implemented regulations governing recommendation algorithms, deepfakes, and generative AI, requiring content labeling and algorithm registration

Organizations that build compliance into their AI systems now will avoid costly retrofits. Those that wait will face the dual burden of meeting requirements and untangling systems that were built without governance in mind.

## AI and Sustainability

AI is both part of the climate problem and part of the solution. Training a single large language model can consume tens of gigawatt-hours of electricity — the International Energy Agency projects that AI data centers could double their energy consumption by 2026.

But AI is also accelerating climate solutions:

- **Energy optimization** — Google DeepMind's AI reduced cooling energy in Google's data centers by 40%. Applied to commercial buildings, similar systems cut energy consumption by 20-30%
- **Weather forecasting** — DeepMind's GraphCast produces 10-day weather forecasts in under a minute that match traditional simulations requiring hours of supercomputer time
- **Grid management** — AI optimizes renewable energy integration by predicting solar and wind output and balancing grid loads in real time

For business leaders, sustainability is becoming an AI governance requirement — not just an ethical consideration. The EU AI Act's documentation requirements include environmental impact disclosures for large-scale AI systems.

## Preparing Your Organization

The leaders who navigate the next wave of AI successfully will share common traits:

- **Foundational readiness** — Clean, accessible data and skilled teams are prerequisites for every emerging AI capability
- **Governance maturity** — Organizations with established AI governance can adopt new capabilities faster because the oversight structures already exist
- **Cultural adaptability** — Teams that have learned to work alongside AI in current applications will adopt next-generation tools with less friction
- **Strategic patience** — Not every trend requires immediate action. Distinguishing between capabilities that are ready for production and those that are still maturing is itself a competitive advantage

> **Key takeaway:** The next wave of AI — agentic systems, small models, multimodal capabilities, and AI-native software — will be adopted fastest by organizations that have already built the foundations: clean data, skilled people, governance frameworks, and a culture that embraces AI as a partner in the work. Start building those foundations now.
